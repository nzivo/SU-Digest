Digest
Beloved Parents and Guardians,

It is our belief that your 2022 year has started well. As the Strathmore University fraternity, we remain grateful that the pandemic seems to have lessened for now, even as we continue to adhere to the Ministry of Health protocols on Covid-19 here on campus.

 

Our theme of this year is devoted to exploring what freedom and responsibility means for all of us, be it students, staff and even you, our parents/guardians.

In this edition, we feature one of our initiatives – the Macheo Program which celebrateds its 10 years’ anniversary in this March. We also feature some activities impacting the education sector in the Teacher Enhancement Programme (TEP), aimed at developing current and future educators. This time, the TEP team carried out a training of teachers in Monrovia, Liberia. In matters partnerships, @iBizAfrica – Strathmore University partnered with Standard Chartered Bank for the 5th cohort of the Women in Tech incubator program. We also salute the Strathmore Law School for facilitating the launch of the Handbook on Prosecuting Conflict Related Sexual Violence. This was an event graced by among others, the Rtd. Lady Justice Joyce Aluoch. In a recent MoU advancing the green and clean energy agenda, the Strathmore Energy Research Centre partnered with Juha Suojanen, Founder and CEO of EkoRent, for the establishment of Kenya’s first Nopea SolarHub Electric Vehicle Charging Station at the centre. Read these and many more, as we move further into the year; and as always, we invite you to keep informed through our website www.strathmore.edu.

We invite you to walk this journey with us, exploring and working to revive our quest for freedom, whilst being responsible in all we undertake. We are convinced that freedom with responsibility is a powerful trait that can benefit our youth in all facets of their lives.
